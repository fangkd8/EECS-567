-- HW1: Pathplanning.

Here are the results of pathplanning.

From these experiments, I found the Greedy-Best-First algorithm is pretty interesting. According to the theory and analyzing its working ways, there would be some possibilities of search failure, but in these simple planning scenes, I have NOT seen any conditions of failure unless the goal points were out of map`s range, which were unaccessible.

With all conditions succeeded, Greedy got the best efficiency of planning, although it has some extreme scene which could possibly cause failure. So I think as for these maps, Greedy-Best-Search is the best searching way indeed.

Besides, while I was coding A* algorithm, I found if I edited the codes of getting current node from shift/push to pop/unshift, some bugs would appear and cause aberrations to the calculation of f_score. Then the searching process would go wrong and the path would be like a snake. The photo of these false paths are really interesting so I manage to get these again and put them in the last paragraph in my report. I believe this phenomenon is related to the order of elements in visit_queue.

From this project, I gained precious experience of coding, this will help me a lot in my future, especially I had barely got access to coding in my undergraduate years.
